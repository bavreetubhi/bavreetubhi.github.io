Heavy-Duty Mobile Workbench

Year 11 Product Design — replaceable top, modular storage, and workshop-ready durability

Overview

This project was a full design-to-build workbench developed around a clear user problem: tools and equipment were being stored on the garage floor with no dedicated workspace or storage system. The goal was to design a workbench that is durable, clean-looking, portable, and highly functional for woodworking and general making.

The final bench combines a large working surface with multi-height drawers and open shelving, and uses a replaceable MDF top with a hardwood protective brim for longevity. The design was constrained by a sub-$500 budget and a build tolerance target of ±5 mm. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Design Brief & Requirements

The bench was designed for a garage environment and long work sessions, with an emphasis on strength, safety, and usability. Key requirements included:

  • Durability: withstand impacts and workshop use without damage
  • Portability: castors for maneuverability and future house moves
  • Replaceable top: removable MDF surface for easy maintenance
  • Storage: multiple drawers + shelves for organised tools/materials
  • Aesthetics: clean, modern look (dark timber contrast preferred)
  • Budget: total cost < $500

Safety and ergonomics were also built in via rounded corners, a fully sanded finish, and workshop-appropriate working height. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

STORAGE & ORGANISATION

Multiple drawers of varying heights plus open shelving for larger tools and materials.

REPLACEABLE TOP

MDF work surface designed to be removable and replaced after wear.

PORTABILITY

Designed for castors to move the bench and lock it securely during work.

Concept Development & Selection

Three design options were evaluated against the project criteria (strength, portability, interchangeable top, sustainability, and garage fit). Option 1 scored highest and was selected due to its balanced layout and functionality. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

The preferred option includes a drawer stack for small-item organisation, open shelves for larger items, and a replaceable top system supported by a robust pine structure. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Final Design & Features

The final bench uses a light-coloured pine frame paired with a darker hardwood perimeter (brim) around the MDF top to protect high-wear edges and create a clean contrasting finish. Drawers use runners for smooth operation, and cut-out drawer pulls keep the front profile simple and snag-free. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

  • Top: removable MDF surface with hardwood protective brim
  • Structure: pine frame designed for strength and workshop loads
  • Storage: multi-height drawers + symmetrical shelving layout
  • Maintainability: replaceable components to extend service life

Materials, Joinery & Finish

The bench was designed around accessible, cost-effective materials and strong joinery. Pine was chosen for the main structure, MDF for the replaceable top, and hardwood for the perimeter wear surface. Assembly used dominos, glue, nails, and screws in a process focused on strength, accuracy, and repeatability. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

MATERIALS

Pine frame + MDF top + hardwood brim chosen for durability, cost, and ease of replacement.

JOINERY

Domino joinery and adhesive bonding used to create strong, square panels and shelves.

FINISH QUALITY

Precision target ±5 mm with sanding and fit checks to ensure clean assembly.

Production Planning & Build Process

The build followed a structured process: panel construction (dominos + glue), shelf fabrication, leg/support assembly, top fitment, hardwood edging, drawer construction, and runner installation. A risk assessment and quality management plan were used to control tool safety and accuracy. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

  • Cutting and laminating shelf panels using dominos + clamps
  • Assembling legs and supports, then fastening to the base structure
  • Cutting and fitting the MDF top and additional reinforcement strips
  • Adding hardwood perimeter with mitres and mechanical fastening
  • Building drawers and installing runners for smooth travel

Testing, Evaluation & Outcome

The bench was evaluated against the original criteria: strength, portability, replaceable top, sustainability, and garage fit. Practical testing included loading and using the bench during construction, and verifying the removable top system and dimensional fit. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

  • Durability: tested with tools and real workshop use; remained sturdy
  • Portability: heavy without castors, designed to roll easily once installed
  • Replaceable top: removable but heavy; future improvement: lighter internal frame
  • Sustainability: low waste; offcuts reused for jigs
  • Garage fit: final dimensions matched the available space

Conclusion

This workbench project demonstrates a full product design workflow: user-centred requirements, concept evaluation, detailed planning, safe production, and criteria-based validation.
