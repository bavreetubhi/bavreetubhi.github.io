Toothless L2 Hybrid Rocket

Level 2 rocket with full custom airframe, composite structures and a student-researched and developed hybrid engine.

Overview

Toothless is a Level 2 high-power rocket designed as a testbed for custom airframe and propulsion hardware. I was responsible for the entire airframe, from CAD through to manufacturing, including a 3D printed and fibreglass-reinforced nose cone, carbon fibre airframe tube, carbon fibre wrapped plywood fins, and a custom SRAD hybrid rocket engine.

The goal was to build a structurally efficient, re-usable platform that could carry the hybrid engine and avionics stack safely through boost, coast and recovery while demonstrating reliable composite manufacturing and integration at the L2 scale.

Build Overview

The build covered everything from CAD and 3D printing through composite layups, fin can assembly and full vehicle integration.

Airframe Architecture

The airframe was designed around a modular layout: nose cone, main carbon fibre body tube, fin can, and separate avionics bay. This made it easier to iterate on individual components without rebuilding the entire rocket.

  • 3D printed PLA or PETG nose cone reinforced with internal foam and external fibreglass skins.
  • Carbon fibre tube forming the main body, laid up over a mandrel for stiffness and low mass.
  • Through-the-wall plywood fins wrapped in carbon fibre, bonded into a fin-can section.
  • Dedicated avionics bay for flight computer, batteries and wiring.

NOSE CONE

3D printed core with fibreglass wrap for impact resistance and surface quality.

CARBON FIBRE TUBE

Composite body tube designed for bending stiffness, low mass and clean outer finish.

FIN CAN

Carbon wrapped plywood fins bonded through the wall into the motor mount for strong load paths.

Nose Cone Design & Build

The nose cone started as a 3D CAD model sized to match the rocket diameter and desired ogive / profile. The core was 3D printed in segments, then joined, sanded and filled to create a smooth plug for glassing.

Multiple layers of fibreglass were applied with epoxy to create a tough, impact resistant shell. The internal volume was reserved for future payloads or tracker hardware, and a bulkhead was added at the shoulder to interface with the recovery system.

Carbon Fibre Tube & Fins

The main body tube was produced by laying up carbon fibre over a cylindrical mandrel. The layup schedule was selected to give sufficient bending stiffness for L2 loads while keeping the wall thin enough to save mass.

Fins were cut from plywood, profiled and then wrapped in carbon fibre to improve stiffness and edge durability. A through-the-wall design tied the fins into the internal motor mount tube to create a robust load path from aerodynamic forces into the airframe.

Material & Layup Details

Different fabrics, resin systems and layup techniques were tested before committing to the final schedule used on the flight article.

SRAD Hybrid Rocket Engine

Toothless was powered by a custom student-researched and developed hybrid engine. The design pulled together injector, combustion chamber, grain geometry and nozzle into a single integrated package sized for a Level 2 flight envelope.

The engine was designed around a safe oxidiser flow rate, regression rate estimates and thrust requirements for the chosen airframe and launch site constraints. Particular care was taken with ignition strategy, sealing and mounting hardware.

Integration & Avionics

The airframe, engine and avionics were integrated through a central motor mount and removable avionics bay. The bay held the flight computer, power system and wiring for pyro channels and tracking, with access ports to arm the system on the pad.

Internal structure was arranged so that thrust, fin loads and recovery forces were all transmitted along clear, reinforced paths, reducing the chance of local failures during boost or deployment.

Testing & Flight

Before flying, the airframe and recovery system were ground tested, including harness tests, ejection charge verification and fit checks of all sections. The hybrid engine underwent separate subsystem checks for plumbing and ignition.

Toothless was then flown as an L2 vehicle, validating the composite airframe, fin can and nose cone, as well as demonstrating the integration of a custom SRAD hybrid on a student-built platform.

Workshop Process

The build involved 3D printing, surface finishing, composite layups, sanding, bonding and repeated fit checks to ensure everything assembled cleanly.

Outcome & Reflection

Toothless consolidated a full stack of skills: 3D CAD, composite manufacturing, hybrid engine design and high-power rocketry integration. Building the rocket from the nose cone down to the fin can and propulsion system gave a much deeper understanding of how each subsystem affects reliability and performance.

The project also provided a stepping stone towards more advanced composite airframes and hybrid propulsion work at higher altitudes and larger scales.
